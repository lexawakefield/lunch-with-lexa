Lunch with Lexa.

A new batch every week. Follow along.

Every Sunday I block out three or four hours, put on something good, and prep my food for the whole week ahead. I call it Lunch with Lexa, and I share the whole thing on Instagram — the recipes, the containers, the colorful batches and the very beige ones.

I'm head over heels for nutrition and fitness right now, and the prep is what ties them together. Good food is the fuel that makes the hard workouts feel possible, and my whole week just goes better when I've set myself up ahead of time.

Three or four hours on a Sunday buys back my whole week. That's the trade I make, every single time.

Lately my training has been shifting, too. I've moved away from running and into cycling and lifting, and I'm a little obsessed — there's something about chasing a heavier lift or a harder ride that keeps pulling me back. That's changed how I eat, as well.

I'm mostly plant-based, so getting enough protein takes real intention, and a lot of my prep these days is basically a protein experiment — beans, lentils, tofu, tempeh, edamame, and whatever else helps me hit my numbers without leaning on meat.
